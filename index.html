Frequently Asked Questions - Car Insurance

Car insurance is a contract between a vehicle owner and an insurance company that provides financial protection in case of an accident, theft, or damage to the vehicle.

The most common types of car insurance coverage are liability insurance, collision insurance, comprehensive insurance, personal injury protection (PIP), and uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage.

Your car insurance needs depend on factors such as the value of your car, your driving habits, and your financial situation. It’s best to talk to an insurance agent to determine the best coverage for your needs.

Liability insurance covers damages to other people or property if you're at fault in an accident. Full coverage typically includes both liability insurance and additional coverage for your own car, such as collision and comprehensive coverage.

The minimum required car insurance coverage varies by state. It’s important to check your state’s requirements, but most states require liability insurance. You may want to consider additional coverage for better protection.

The cost of car insurance depends on several factors, including your driving record, the type of car you drive, your age, and your location. On average, Americans pay around $1,500 per year for car insurance, but rates can vary widely.

You can lower your car insurance premiums by maintaining a clean driving record, increasing your deductibles, bundling your insurance policies, or taking advantage of discounts such as good student or multi-vehicle discounts.

A deductible is the amount you pay out-of-pocket for damages before your insurance coverage kicks in. Generally, the higher your deductible, the lower your premium, but you’ll pay more if you need to file a claim.

If you don’t own a car but drive frequently, you may need non-owner car insurance, which provides liability coverage when you drive someone else's car.

Yes, you can cancel your car insurance at any time, but be sure to check for cancellation fees. It's important to ensure you have another insurance policy in place before canceling, to avoid driving without coverage.

To file a claim, contact your insurance company as soon as possible after an accident or damage. Provide all relevant details, including the police report (if applicable), and cooperate with the claims adjuster.

It depends on your policy. Some car insurance policies cover rental cars under certain conditions, while others may not. You can opt for rental car coverage as an add-on to your policy or purchase it from the rental company.
